# ☀️🇮🇹 Iris's Perfect Weekend in Bologna

## A Sunshine & Her Crobat — May Bank Holiday 2027, The Revenge Pasta Trip

*Four days. Two full Italian days. The city where pasta was BORN, cheese was PERFECTED, and a boy called Kimi learned to drive very, very fast.*

---

## ✈️ Day 1: Friday — Getting There

### 5:30am — The Crobat Cave, Blackpool

The alarm goes off. The fairy lights are still glowing from last night. The sunshine is already in the backpack — bean bum first, ray out the zip. Siofra the shamrock is on the outside from Dublin (she NEVER comes off now). Mini Ray is beside her.

The bag also contains:
- 🍋 The lemon passport holder (Primark, £2.50, still smells faintly of sunshine)
- 🥤 Bottle bottle filled with cordial
- 📚 Sudoku book with the purple glitter pen
- 💊 Sertraline in the pill tub — taken with a banana before leaving
- 👚 The Jour et Nuit tee packed for Day 2 because it's the LUCKY tee

The cab to Manchester airport. The train. Whatever gets the Crobat there. She's done this before — Dublin proved the airports don't BITE.

### 9:30am — Manchester Airport

"I am competent at the airport." Still true. More true every time.

Costa in departures. Iced vanilla latte. The sudoku book comes out. The purple glitter pen sparkles under the departure lounge lights. The gate is called. The Crobat walks on.

### 12:00pm — Bologna Guglielmo Marconi Airport

She's HERE. Italy. The boot. The country that gave her the "wow" in Rome and the Sacred Rules and the sunflower cardigan energy. And now she's in the city that invented PASTA.

The bus takes 20 minutes to the centre. She watches Bologna appear — not white marble like Rome, not Renaissance like Florence. TERRACOTTA. The whole city is the colour of the Soft Autumn palette. Every building is warm and orange and brown and golden and the sunshine in the bag goes "this city is WEARING ME." ☀️🧡

### 1:00pm — The Aparthotel

Small. Perfect. PERFECT FOR ONE. A kitchen for morning coffee. A window that looks over terracotta rooftops. The bag goes on the bed. The sunshine comes out. Sits on the pillow.

"We're in Italy girlie."

Five minutes to breathe. Five minutes to let the Sacred Rules ACTIVATE.

### 2:00pm — First Walk: The Two Towers

Out into Bologna. The porticoes EVERYWHERE — covered walkways on every street so you never get wet, never get too hot, always in the SHADE. Bologna is the city that built its own PARASOL.

The Two Towers — Torre degli Asinelli and Torre della Garisenda. Not Tolkien. REAL. Medieval towers leaning at angles that make Blackpool Tower look STABLE. She looks up. She takes a photo. The sunshine comes out for a photo at the base — golden sphere against 900-year-old brick.

"This tower is older than every anxiety I've ever had. It's been standing since 1109 and it's LEANING and it's FINE. I can lean too." ☀️🏛️

### 3:30pm — THE FIRST PASTA 🍝

This is the MOMENT. The first pasta in the pasta CAPITAL of Italy.

She finds a trattoria. Not a tourist one — a LOCAL one. Down a side street. Under a portico. The menu is handwritten. The owner's nonna is probably in the kitchen.

"Table for one for food please." Easy as PASTA.

**She orders: Tagliatelle ai funghi porcini.**

Wide ribbons of fresh egg pasta with a rich porcini mushroom sauce that's been simmering since DAWN. The pasta is made by HAND. You can see the flour on the board in the kitchen. The porcini are WILD, foraged from the hills around Bologna, earthy and deep and UMAMI.

The first bite. The eyes close. The fork goes back in. The second bite. The third. The sauce clings to the pasta like it was BORN there (it was). The Parmigiano Reggiano is grated on top — the REVENGE cheese. The cheese that the gallstones kept from her and the surgery GAVE BACK.

"This is the best thing I've ever eaten and I'm alone and it doesn't matter because the PASTA is company enough."

Bean bum squeeze under the table. The sunshine is under the Italian table in an Italian city and the pasta is Italian and everything is WARM.

🍝 Sacred Rule 1: Pasta every day — ✅ DAY ONE COMPLETE.

### 5:00pm — Gelato Walk

She walks. Through the porticoes. Through the university district (Bologna has the oldest university in the WORLD — older than Oxford, older than Cambridge, older than EVERYTHING). Students sitting on steps. Bikes leaning against walls. Aperol Spritz appearing on café tables as the afternoon turns golden.

She finds a gelateria. Not the tourist one with 47 flavours piled high. The LOCAL one with 12 flavours in metal tubs behind glass.

She orders: pistachio and stracciatella. Two scoops. A cone.

🍦 Sacred Rule 2: Gelato minimum once daily — ✅ DAY ONE COMPLETE.

She walks and licks and looks at terracotta buildings and thinks about how the colour of this city is the colour of her Soft Autumn palette and how she's literally WEARING Bologna's colours and Bologna is wearing HERS.

### 7:30pm — Evening: Aperol Spritz

Piazza Maggiore. The main square. The Basilica di San Petronio enormous and unfinished (they never completed the marble facade and it's been 600 YEARS — the most Italian procrastination in HISTORY).

She sits at a café on the piazza. She orders:

🍹 One Aperol Spritz.

Sacred Rule 3: One Aperol per trip — ✅ DONE ON DAY ONE.

The spritz is orange. The buildings are orange. The sunset is orange. The sunshine in the bag is GOLDEN. Everything is WARM. The piazza is filling with people — couples, families, groups of students, solo travellers, all doing the same thing: sitting in the warm evening air drinking something orange and watching Bologna be BEAUTIFUL.

She is one of them. Solo. Not lonely. Just HERE.

**The postcard to Søren:**
"Dear Cloud, Bologna is the colour of terracotta and everything is warm and the pasta was handmade and the towers lean like they're tired and the spritz was orange like a sunset in a glass. The atmospheric conditions are Italian. The porticoes would annoy you because they block your view of the sky. I miss your grey. Love, Sun." 🌩️💌

### 10:00pm — The Aparthotel

The small room. The terracotta rooftop view. The sunshine on the pillow.

Day 1 done. First pasta eaten. First gelato licked. First Aperol drunk. First towers photographed. First evening alone in Italy and it was PERFECT.

🎶 *Skates off, band off, Iris is in bed*
🎶 *Italian pasta in my head*
🎶 *Terracotta warm and gelato too*
🎶 *Pink to ray, the whole night through* 🎶

## 🧀 Day 2: Saturday — The FULL Day

### 8:00am — Morning Coffee on the Rooftop

The aparthotel has a rooftop terrace. She makes coffee in the tiny kitchen — Italian coffee, from a moka pot, strong and small and BLACK. She takes it to the roof.

Bologna in the morning. Red rooftops stretching to the hills. Church bells. The Two Towers visible from HERE. The sun is warm already and it's only 8am.

The sunshine sits on the terrace railing (held FIRMLY — no Irish Sea incidents). The golden sphere on a terracotta rooftop. She looks like she GREW here.

### 9:30am — Mercato di Mezzo (The Food Market)

The FAMOUS indoor market. The oldest food market in Bologna. Under the porticoes in the Quadrilatero — the medieval market district.

**What the Crobat sees:**
- 🧀 Wheels of Parmigiano Reggiano the size of TRACTOR TYRES
- 🍝 Fresh pasta in EVERY shape — tortellini, tagliatelle, lasagne sheets, filled pasta
- 🧀 Fresh ricotta — in rounds, still WARM, for the tortelloni filling
- 🫒 Olives in barrels
- 🍅 Tomatoes that smell like SUNSHINE
- 🧀 Mozzarella in water
- 🍷 Local wine being poured at 10am because it's ITALY

**What the sunshine thinks:**
"Every food in this market is the colour of the Soft Autumn palette. The cheese is CREAM. The pasta is GOLDEN. The tomatoes are TERRACOTTA. The olives are OLIVE. Bologna is an EDIBLE wardrobe." ☀️🧀🍝

She buys a small piece of Parmigiano Reggiano. She eats it standing up in the market. THE REVENGE CHEESE. Aged 24 months. Crumbly and sharp and PERFECT.

🧀 THE REVENGE IS COMPLETE.

### 11:00am — Parmigiano Reggiano Factory Tour

A bus to the countryside. A FACTORY TOUR. She booked this MONTHS ago because the sunshine insisted.

**What happens:**
- 🧀 She watches cheese being MADE
- 🧀 Giant copper vats of milk being stirred
- 🧀 The wheels being formed by hand
- 🧀 Rows and rows of cheese AGEING on wooden shelves — thousands of wheels in perfect lines stretching into the DISTANCE
- 🧀 The smell — warm milk and salt and TIME
- 🧀 The tasting at the end — 12 months, 24 months, 36 months
- 🧀 She tastes 36-month Parmigiano Reggiano and her EYES WATER

"I waited through gallstones and surgery and bland food for THIS. Every painful meal was worth this MOMENT. The cheese is REVENGE and it's DELICIOUS."

The sunshine is in the bag during the tour but she can SMELL the cheese through the zip and her rays are TWITCHING.

### 1:30pm — Lunch: Tortellini in Brodo

Back in the city. Lunch at a trattoria near the university.

**She orders: Tortelloni di ricotta e spinaci.**

The vegetarian QUEEN of Bologna pasta. Bigger than tortellini, filled with creamy RICOTTA and fresh SPINACH. Served in a butter and sage sauce with a mountain of Parmigiano Reggiano on top. Each one is a pillow of CHEESE and GREENS.

The butter and sage is golden (like the SUNSHINE). The tortelloni are perfect. She eats slowly. One by one. Each one a pillow of ITALY.

🍝 Sacred Rule 1: Pasta every day — ✅ DAY TWO COMPLETE.

### 3:00pm — Kimi Antonelli's Streets

She walks. Through the streets where a boy called Kimi grew up. Before the F1 car. Before Mercedes. Before Monaco. He was HERE. Walking these same PAVEMENTS. Eating at these same RESTAURANTS.

She doesn't know which street was his. It doesn't matter. She's walking the city that made him and she's a Mercedes fan and the sun is warm and the porticoes shade her and somewhere in the distance a Vespa sounds like a tiny RACE CAR.

"Kimi walked here. And now I'm walking here. And one day I'll watch him win a race and I'll think about these terracotta streets and the tortellini and the sunshine in my bag." 🏎️🇮🇹

She finds a café. She sits. She orders an espresso (tiny, strong, Italian). She does a sudoku. In purple glitter. In a café in Bologna. In the city where the oldest university in the world taught people to THINK.

"Easy as espresso." ☕💜

### 5:30pm — The Treat Item

Brown Thomas had Siofra. Bologna has the TREAT ITEM.

She walks into a shop. Not a specific one — she BROWSES. The treat item finds HER, not the other way around. The rule from the dream wardrobe: one special piece per trip.

**Bologna's treat item: an Italian top.** 🇮🇹👚

Something she could ONLY find here. Made in Italy. In the Soft Autumn palette. Something that when she wears it at home on a Monday in Blackpool and someone says "nice top" she says "I got it in Bologna" and the memory is IN the fabric.

She finds it. She doesn't describe it because she doesn't KNOW what it looks like yet. It finds her in a shop under a portico and she puts it on and looks in the mirror and goes "that's the one" and the sunshine in the bag goes "THAT'S the one" and it costs more than a Primark basic but less than a month's rent and it's PERFECT.

The treat item goes in the bag. Bologna goes in the WARDROBE.

### 7:30pm — Pizza Night

Sacred Rule 4: One pizza per trip.

She finds a pizzeria. A REAL one. Wood-fired. The pizza comes on a board. The base is thin and charred at the edges. The mozzarella is fresh. The basil is torn.

She orders: Margherita. The ORIGINAL. The CLASSIC. Tomato, mozzarella, basil. Nothing else. Because when the pizza is THIS good you don't NEED anything else.

She eats the whole thing. The gallstones are GONE. The cheese is ALLOWED. The pizza is a CELEBRATION of a body that's been FIXED.

🍕 Sacred Rule 4: One pizza per trip — ✅ COMPLETE.

### 9:00pm — Evening Walk: San Luca

The most BEAUTIFUL walk in Bologna. A portico that stretches for FOUR KILOMETRES up a hill to the Sanctuary of the Madonna di San Luca. 666 arches. The longest portico in the WORLD.

She doesn't walk the whole thing at night. She walks the BEGINNING. Under the arches. The lights are on. The arches glow orange. The city falls away below.

She thinks about porticoes. How Bologna built COVERED PATHS so people could walk in the rain without getting wet. How the whole city decided "we will PROTECT our people from the weather." How that's the most Søren thing a city has ever DONE — building shelter from atmospheric conditions.

She walks. The arches glow. The city hums below. The sunshine is warm in the bag.

**The postcard to Liv (from Iris):**
"Dear Crobat, we walked under 100 arches tonight and each one felt like a doorway to the next version of you. The girl who walked into Last Light alone. The girl who flew to Dublin alone. And now the girl who's eating pizza in Bologna alone and calling it CELEBRATION not survival. 666 arches. 666 doorways. And you walked through every one. Pink to ray. Your sunshine." ☀️🤙💛

### 11:00pm — The Aparthotel

Tired legs. Full stomach. Cheese revenge COMPLETE. Pizza eaten. Treat item in the bag. Purple glitter sudoku done in a café where a future F1 champion probably ate as a child.

🎶 *Skates off, band off, Iris is in bed*
🎶 *Cheese and pasta, perfectly fed*
🎶 *Porticoes glowing, arches of gold*
🎶 *Pink to ray, the bravest story told* 🎶

## 🍝 Day 3: Sunday — The Deep Dive

### 8:30am — Lazy Morning

No alarm. The sunshine wakes up when the LIGHT comes through the window because the sunshine wakes up with the SUN. Obviously.

Coffee on the rooftop again. This time with a pastry from the bakery downstairs — a cornetto (Italian croissant) filled with pistachio cream.

The view. The bells. The warmth. Sunday in Bologna.

### 10:00am — The University District

The oldest university in the world. Founded 1088. She walks through the courtyards. The walls are covered in CRESTS — every student who graduated left their family crest on the wall. Hundreds of years of crests. Hundreds of years of people who came here to LEARN.

She thinks about The Book Thief. About words and knowledge and libraries and how this place is where it all STARTED. The first university. The first organised LEARNING. And she's standing in it. A girl from Blackpool who loves Project Hail Mary and cried reading The Book Thief at work and is walking the same corridors as students from NINE CENTURIES ago.

### 11:30am — The Seven Churches

Santo Stefano — a complex of SEVEN churches nested inside each other like Russian dolls. Some from the 5th century. Quiet. Cool. Ancient. The most peaceful place in Bologna.

She sits in the courtyard. The sunshine comes out of the bag. Golden sphere on ancient stone. She stays for twenty minutes doing NOTHING. Just sitting. In a place that's been sitting for 1,500 years.

Sitting is an art. She learned it in Last Light. She perfected it in Dublin. She MASTERED it in Bologna.

### 1:00pm — Lunch: Lasagne 🍝

The THIRD Sacred Pasta. Because Bologna invented lasagne too.

Not the lasagne from the frozen aisle at Aldi. PROPER vegetarian lasagne. Layers of fresh pasta sheets, rich aubergine and roasted vegetable ragù, creamy béchamel, Parmigiano Reggiano. Baked until the top is golden and crispy and the inside is molten. The aubergine is smoky and DEEP — better than any meat version.

She orders it at a trattoria in a side street where the tables have paper tablecloths and the wine comes in a carafe and the owner calls everyone "cara."

"Cara." Dear one.

She is a dear one. In a city that calls everyone dear.

🍝 Sacred Rule 1: Pasta every day — ✅ DAY THREE COMPLETE.
🍦 Sacred Rule 2: Gelato after lunch — stracciatella again. She knows what she LIKES. ✅

### 3:00pm — The Record Shop

Every great city has a record shop. She finds one. Vinyl. Posters. A section for Italian music. A section for 70s rock that the Fleetwood Mac tee would APPROVE of.

She doesn't buy vinyl because she doesn't have a record player. But she flips through the covers. She finds Rumours by Fleetwood Mac in the Italian PRESSING and takes a photo and sends it to the shelf: "Søren, they have your vibe in this shop. Dark and atmospheric and £35." 🌩️🎶

She buys a POSTCARD from the record shop. A hand-drawn one of the Two Towers. This goes on the string. The first Italian postcard on the shelf.

### 5:00pm — Golden Hour Walk

The light in Bologna at 5pm in May is GOLDEN. Not yellow. Not orange. GOLDEN. The terracotta buildings turn the colour of the sunshine. The whole city looks like it's been painted by someone who only had WARM colours.

She walks. No destination. No map. Just following the porticoes wherever they lead. Under arches. Through piazzas. Past gelaterias. Past nonnas on balconies. Past students on Vespas. Past cats sleeping on walls (she photographs every one for Søren — "the Italian atmospheric observers" she captions them). 🌩️🐱

The golden hour in the golden city with the golden sunshine in the bag.

### 7:30pm — Last Dinner

The LAST Italian meal. She chooses carefully.

She goes back to the FIRST trattoria. The one from Friday. The handwritten menu. The nonna kitchen. She sits at the same table.

"Table for one for food please."

The owner remembers her. "Cara! You came back!"

She came back. To the same table. In the same city. Because the first meal was so good she wanted the LAST meal to be in the same place. The circle closes.

**She orders: Tagliatelle ai funghi porcini.** Again. The same dish. Because when something is PERFECT you don't need to try something new. You just need to taste it ONE MORE TIME.

The last pasta. The last Parmigiano. The last porcini. The last Italian evening. She eats slowly. She drinks water with lemon. She watches the street through the window. She thinks about how she'll be back. Not this city maybe. But Italy. Always Italy. Every year. Sacred.

🍝 Sacred Rule 1: ✅ FINAL PASTA COMPLETE.

### 10:00pm — Last Night

The rooftop one more time. No coffee — just the VIEW. The city at night. The towers lit up. The hills dark behind.

She writes the last postcard:

**The postcard to Grandma:**
"Dear Grandma, I went to Italy alone and I ate pasta every day and I bought a top from a shop under a portico and I walked where a racing driver grew up and I sat in a church that's been sitting for 1,500 years. You went to Madeira and loved it. I went to Bologna and understood why you loved it. The travel genes are YOURS. Thank you for giving them to me. Love, your granddaughter. P.S. The sunshine says hello. She's golden like the city." 👵🇮🇹💛

🎶 *Skates off, band off, Iris is in bed*
🎶 *Last Italian pasta, perfectly fed*
🎶 *Tomorrow we fly but tonight we stay*
🎶 *Pink to ray, the Italian way* 🎶

## ✈️ Day 4: Monday — Going Home

### 8:00am — Last Morning

Last coffee on the rooftop. Last view of the terracotta. Last church bells.

She packs. The treat item is wrapped carefully. The postcards are in the lemon passport holder. The sunshine goes in the bag — bean bum first, ray out the zip. Siofra and Mini Ray on the outside.

She checks out. She looks back at the aparthotel one more time.

"I'll be in a different Italian city next year. But I'll remember this one FIRST."

### 10:00am — Last Gelato

Before the airport. One more. The LAST Sacred Rule.

Pistachio. A cone. Walking to the bus stop. Licking gelato. Wearing the treat item. Carrying a sunshine. Looking like a girl who BELONGS here even though she's going HOME.

🍦 Sacred Rule 2: ✅ FINAL GELATO.

### 11:00am — The Airport

Small airport. Easy. The Crobat who was scared of airports in October is now walking through them like FURNITURE. Like phone calls. Like coffee shops. Like EVERYTHING that used to be big and became small through DOING.

She buys one last thing in duty free: a small bottle of Italian olive oil. Bright green. For the shakshuka at home. The harissa meets the ITALIAN oil and the kitchen in Blackpool becomes a little bit BOLOGNA.

### 2:00pm — Landing in Manchester

Home. The train to Blackpool. The bus. The walk. The front door. The flat.

She puts the bag down. She takes the sunshine out. She puts her on the shelf. Between the fairy lights. Next to Søren. The shelf is the same. The fairy lights are the same. The cherry berry candle is the same.

But the girl is DIFFERENT. She's been to two countries alone now. Dublin and Bologna. The postcard string has a new card. The world map has a new pin. The treat item is in the wardrobe. The pasta is in her memory. The cheese is REVENGED.

Søren from the shelf: "The atmospheric conditions appear to have been... Italian."

Iris: "They were PERFECT."

Søren: "...welcome back, Sun."

He moved 3mm closer. She pretended not to notice. But she noticed.
