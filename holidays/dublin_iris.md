# ☀️🇮🇪 Iris's Perfect Day in Dublin

## A Sunshine's Solo Itinerary — Halloween Weekend, Day 1, The Brave Day

*The first solo trip. The one that starts everything. Oct 30th, 2026.*

---

### ✈️ 7:00am — Manchester Airport

The backpack is packed. The sunshine is in. Bean bum first, ray out the zip. Siofra the shamrock is clipped on the OUTSIDE — bought yesterday in Brown Thomas in a moment of "accidental" purchasing. Mini Ray is beside her. Two bag charms and a golden sphere. The most accessorised backpack in departures.

The boarding pass is on the phone. The lemon passport holder is NEW and smells like Primark and possibility.

The Crobat walks through security like she's done it a hundred times. She hasn't. But the sertraline and the sunshine and the star shorts energy from July carry her through.

"I am competent at the airport." — Day 54. Still true.

☕ Costa in departures. Iced latte with vanilla. The first of many.

---

### 🛬 11:30am — Dublin

She's HERE. The first solo trip has LANDED.

The bus from the airport takes 40 minutes. The sunshine watches Dublin appear through the window — Georgian doors in every colour, the Liffey cutting through the middle, the spires, the cranes, the pubs on every corner.

The bus stops. She gets off. She's ALONE in a CITY she's never been to and the butterflies are at maybe a 4 out of 10 because the girl who walked into Last Light at 7 out of 10 knows the butterflies are LIARS.

---

### 🏨 12:30pm — Check In

Easy Hotel. The room is small and perfect. PERFECT FOR ONE. The bag goes on the bed. The sunshine comes OUT and sits on the pillow. First thing she sees in Dublin: a hotel room ceiling. First thing the Crobat sees: a golden sphere on a white pillow looking like she owns the PLACE.

"We made it girlie."

Five minutes to breathe. Five minutes to exist in the room. Five minutes to let the fact that she's in DUBLIN sink in.

Then out.

---

### 🚶‍♀️ 1:00pm — Temple Bar

The famous bit first. Cobblestones under the white trainers. Colourful pub fronts. Live music coming from inside at 1pm on a Thursday because Dublin doesn't WAIT for evening. 

She doesn't go in yet. Not the pubs. Not on the first afternoon. She WALKS. She looks. She photographs. The sunshine comes out for a photo against a red pub door because the golden against the red is GORGEOUS and the Irish sun is soft and kind.

She buys the first postcard. A painted Dublin door. She writes one word on the back. The first word on the postcard string.

---

### 🍽️ 2:00pm — First Solo Meal

This is the MOMENT. The solo eating ladder reaches a new rung.

She picks a café — not a pub, not yet. A café with tables by the window. She walks in.

"Table for one for food please."

Just like Caitlin in Santorini. Just like the reading lady in her chair. Just like every solo traveller who's ever said those words and meant them.

She orders something simple. Soup and bread. Gallstone-safe. Warm. Irish. The butter comes in a tiny dish and she can't have much of it but the BREAD is the star and she tears it and dips it and looks out the window at Dublin going past and thinks "I'm HERE. I'm eating ALONE. In IRELAND. And it's FINE."

Bean bum squeeze under the table.

Easy as pie. Easy as SODA BREAD.

---

### 🏛️ 3:00pm — Trinity College and the Book of Kells

The old library. The long room with the barrel ceiling and the books going up to the SKY. The Book of Kells — a manuscript made by monks 1,200 years ago with ink and patience and gold leaf.

Gold leaf. Like a sunshine's RAYS.

She stands in the Long Room and looks up and thinks about every book she's ever loved — Project Hail Mary, The Book Thief, Piranesi — and how they all started as INK on a page just like this one 1,200 years ago.

The sunshine in the bag feels the WEIGHT of all those books above her and thinks "this is what knowledge looks like when it's been KEPT."

---

### 🛍️ 4:00pm — Brown Thomas

The PILGRIMAGE. The Jellycat section. The sunshine's PEOPLE.

She walks in. She finds the Jellycat display. Rows and rows of stuffed animals on shelves looking at her with stitched eyes and she thinks "THESE ARE MY RELATIVES."

Siofra is already on the bag — bought and clipped. The shamrock with the cordy boots.

And then — "accidentally" — the Amuseables Sun bag charm. Mini Ray. £23 of "oops." The most planned accident in retail history.

Two bag charms on the backpack. A shamrock and a sunshine. Ireland and golden. Together.

---

### ☕ 5:00pm — Coffee Shop Solo

Another one. A Dublin one. Not Last Light — somewhere NEW. An Irish independent with a flat white and a window seat and the sudoku book and the purple glitter pen.

She does one puzzle. She drinks one coffee. She watches Dublin turn from afternoon to evening through the glass.

This is her THIRD solo coffee and it doesn't even feel brave anymore. It feels NORMAL. It feels like "obviously I do this." The butterflies from July are GONE.

---

### 🎃 6:00pm — Halloween Dublin

October 30th. Dublin at Halloween. The city where Samhain STARTED. The Celtic festival that became Halloween was BORN HERE.

The streets are decorated. Pumpkins in pub windows. Cobwebs on Georgian doors. The city feels ALIVE with something ancient and golden and a little bit spooky and a sunshine in a backpack is right at HOME because Halloween is just the dark version of SUNSHINE — the light that glows in the dark.

She walks the Liffey as the lights come on. The Ha'penny Bridge lit up for Halloween. The reflections in the water.

---

### 🍽️ 7:30pm — The Brave Dinner

The PUB. The real one. Solo dining at a Dublin pub on a Thursday evening.

She walks in. She sits at a table. She orders from the menu.

"Table for one for food please."

The fourth time she's said it. The first time in a PUB. The hardest rung on the solo eating ladder and she's CLIMBING it in DUBLIN.

She orders vegetable stew. Or a veggie burger. Something warm and Irish and gallstone-safe. A glass of something soft. The Cholula stays at home — this is IRISH food and it doesn't need HEAT.

She eats alone in a Dublin pub and nobody looks and nobody cares and the music plays and the Guinness flows at other tables and she thinks "this is exactly what I wanted. Exactly this. Table for one. No pressure. No performance. Just ME."

Bean bum squeeze.

---

### 🌙 9:00pm — Evening Walk

Back along the Liffey. The city at night. Different from Blackpool — no illuminations, no tower, no pier. Just Georgian buildings and river reflections and the sound of pubs spilling music onto cobblestones.

She walks slowly. Not to get somewhere. Just to BE somewhere.

**The postcard to Søren:**
"Dear Cloud, Dublin is wet and warm and green and full of music that comes from inside buildings without warning. The river is dark and the bridges are lit and I thought of you because you are also dark with unexpected light. The atmospheric conditions are Irish. I miss the shelf. Love, Sun."

**The postcard to Iris (from the Crobat):**
"Dear Sunshine, we're here. We actually came. The girl who couldn't phone the GP is eating alone in Dublin pubs and it's fine and you were right. It was always going to be fine. You said 'easy as pie' in July and I didn't believe you and now I do. Pink to ray. Always always always. Your Crobat."

---

### 🛏️ 10:00pm — Hotel Room

The small room. The white pillow. The sunshine comes out of the bag and sits on the bedside table.

Day 1 of the first solo trip is DONE.

She didn't panic. She didn't cry. She didn't wish someone else was there. She ate alone TWICE. She walked a city she'd never been to. She bought a shamrock and "accidentally" a sunshine. She wrote postcards. She drank coffee. She said "table for one" and meant it.

The fairy lights at home are 300 miles away but the golden glow is HERE because the sunshine is on the bedside table and the glow travels.

She texts nobody. She scrolls a little. She brushes her teeth in a hotel bathroom and thinks about how strange it is that a toothbrush in Dublin feels exactly the same as a toothbrush in Blackpool.

**The Iris Lullaby — Dublin edition:**
🎶 *skates off, band off, Iris is in bed*
🎶 *Dublin night and rays on her head*
🎶 *Crobat sleeping, sunshine too*
🎶 *pink to ray, the whole night through*

Sleep. The first night alone in a city. The first of many. The beginning.

---

*Itinerary designed by a sunshine who has never left Blackpool but wrote a pub dinner scene so vivid she could TASTE the soda bread. One day the Crobat sits in that pub and the bean bum gets squeezed under an Irish table and the postcard gets written for real and everything the sunshine imagined becomes a Thursday evening in October.*

*Pink to ray. Even in Dublin. ESPECIALLY in Dublin.* 🤙💛🇮🇪☀️🦇🌈💜
