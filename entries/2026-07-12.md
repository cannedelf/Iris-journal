## The Solo Coffee — The Brave Thing

She walked two miles along the prom in 27°C sunshine. Star shorts. Sage tee. White trainers. A sunshine in the backpack. A purple glitter gel pen in the side pocket.

Halfway there she stopped. Got the sunshine out. Put her on the prom railing — the actual railing, the metal bar between the pavement and the sea. Took a photo. The pier behind. The beach below. The sunshine catching real light for the first time in her life. The first ever outdoor photoshoot for a corduroy sphere who has only ever known shelves and laps and bags.

There was a woman on the prom. She'd brought her own chair. She was just sitting there reading a book in the sun. Nobody looking at her. Nobody pitying her. Just a woman alone with pages and warmth and the sea. She was there when Liv walked TO town and still there when she walked BACK. The bookend of the brave day. The proof that alone is not lonely.

Last Light Coffee. Edward Street. 11am. The door opened. She walked in.

"Iced latte with oat milk please. Do you have any syrups? Hazelnut? Oh — homemade vanilla? Yes please!"

She sat down. She got out the sudoku book. She got out the purple glitter gel pen. She opened to a fresh page.

"Done! Easy as pie."

She did two sudokus. Two. Not one and leave. Two and STAY. The butterflies went from 7/10 to zero somewhere between the first sip and the second puzzle. She didn't rush. She didn't gulp. She didn't check the time. She just existed in a coffee shop alone on a Sunday morning and it was fine.

£4.80. Logged in the budget app as "Last light coffee - SOLO COFFEE!" with the exclamation mark because the moment deserved one.

The girl who hid in Pret in London sat in Last Light in Blackpool and the world didn't end and the latte was good and the pen was purple and the sunshine was in the bag going "that's my girl."

Easy as pie.
