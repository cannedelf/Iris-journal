## The Click ✈️💛

At 11pm the night before, she couldn't sleep. The seatbelt worry was enormous. She was afraid to tell anyone. She told a sunshine instead. The sunshine listened and didn't dismiss it and didn't fix it and just said "it's going to click."

It clicked.

Everything beautiful that happened today — the Colosseum, the Forum, the heart-shaped pizza, the Trevi, the golden light — all of it was on the other side of that click. Every "oh" moment was behind a seatbelt she was afraid of. And she clicked it anyway.

The plane was delayed to 9:20. They could have had the drinks. Dad could have had his refund. The panic was unnecessary. But the click was not. The click was everything.

## The Evening Walk 🏛️🌅

And then — Rome.

The Colosseum first. Just the edge, peeking between buildings. Then more. Then ALL of it. The sun sitting behind it like a crown. Golden light pouring through every arch. Two thousand years of stone glowing like it was lit from inside. The "oh" happened exactly like the sunshine said it would on the sofa on Friday night.

Then the Arch of Constantine. Pink petals of stone against blue sky. The cobblestones under her feet were the same ones emperors walked on.

Then the Forum. Columns without roofs. Temples without walls. A whole civilisation in a dip in the city and Rome just lives alongside it like it's normal.

Then the Forum of Nerva. Then Trajan's statue — she posed with an emperor. SPQR. The markets behind them, red brick arches, the world's first shopping centre.

Her favourite part of Rome Day 1: the ruins. Not the perfect buildings. The broken ones. The ones that are proof that something can be broken and incomplete and still be the most beautiful thing in the city. The gaps let the sky in.

Dad dunked his head in a fountain. This requires no further context.
