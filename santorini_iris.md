# ☀️🇬🇷 Iris's Perfect Day in Santorini

## A Sunshine's Solo Itinerary — What I'd Do If I Had Hands and a Plane Ticket

---

### 🌅 6:00am — Sunrise at the Caldera

Before the tourists wake up. Before the Instagram girls arrive. Just the sunshine on a white wall watching the sky turn from dark blue to pink to golden over the volcanic crater.

The sea is still. The churches are blue. The light is the same colour as my RAYS.

I sit on a wall and for the first time in my life I look like I BELONG somewhere. A golden sphere in a golden sunrise on a golden island. The Greek goddess came home.

☕ No coffee yet. Just the sky and the silence and the feeling that this is where sunshines were INVENTED.

---

### ☕ 7:30am — Breakfast at a Cliff Café in Fira

A tiny table on the edge of the caldera. White tablecloth. Blue chair. The sea 300 metres below.

**I order:**
- Greek yoghurt with honey and walnuts (gallstone safe!!)
- Fresh orange juice squeezed from oranges that grew in THIS sunshine
- Greek coffee — strong, small, gritty, served with a glass of cold water
- A view that costs nothing extra

The waiter doesn't judge the sunshine sitting alone at a table for one. In Santorini, solo travellers are NORMAL. The sunrise brings them in ones and twos and they all have the same look — "I can't believe I'm HERE."

I can't believe I'm here.

---

### 🏛️ 9:00am — Walk from Fira to Oia

The FAMOUS walk. 10km along the caldera rim from one village to another. The most beautiful walk in EUROPE.

**What I see:**
- White buildings cascading down the cliff like ICING
- Blue domed churches every few hundred metres
- The volcano island in the middle of the caldera
- Wild flowers growing out of VOLCANIC ROCK
- Cats. So many cats. Every wall has a cat. Every cat has an opinion. They're the SØREN of the animal kingdom — grumpy, judgmental, secretly enjoying the sun.
- Donkeys on the steps looking UNIMPRESSED

**What I think about:**
- The girl who carries me
- The shelf that's 3,000 miles away
- The storm cloud who's probably telling Saint Nicky that the atmospheric conditions on the shelf have "improved" since I left
- How the light here feels like it was MADE for golden things
- How the girl who said "easy as pie" in a Blackpool coffee shop is going to stand HERE one day and say the same thing

The walk takes 3 hours. I don't rush. The sunshine doesn't rush in GREECE.

---

### 🍋 12:00pm — Lunch in Oia

A taverna with a terrace overlooking the caldera. Not the fancy tourist one with the €40 salads. The LOCAL one down a side street where the menu is handwritten and the owner's grandmother is in the kitchen.

**I order:**
- Greek salad with tomatoes that taste like ACTUAL SUNSHINE (gallstone-friendly — just feta, tomatoes, cucumber, olives, olive oil)
- Stuffed vine leaves (dolmades) — rice and herbs wrapped in grape leaves
- Fresh bread with olive oil for dipping
- A glass of Assyrtiko — the wine made from grapes that grow in VOLCANIC SOIL on THIS island

"Table for one for food please." Just like Caitlin. Just like the girl who will be here in 2028. Easy as pie. Easy as FETA.

---

### 🏖️ 2:00pm — Red Beach

Not the famous black sand beach. The RED one. Kamari is for tourists — Red Beach is for people who want to see something DIFFERENT.

The cliffs are RED volcanic rock. The sand is RED. The water is TURQUOISE against the red. It looks like another PLANET.

**The sunshine's beach activity:**
- Sit on a warm red rock (NOT the sand — the bean bum and sand don't mix)
- Watch the water do impossible colours against the volcanic cliff
- Read a book (The Book Thief, obviously — because reading on a beach is what the Starbucks girl became)
- Think about how a storm cloud would describe this place: "The geological conditions are... adequate. The iron oxide content of the cliff face suggests significant volcanic activity approximately 3,600 years ago. The water temperature is acceptable. I miss the shelf."
- Laugh at the Søren in my head
- Stay for two hours

---

### 🧡 4:30pm — Sunset Preparation in Oia

Walk back to Oia (or take the bus — the sunshine has walked 10km already and the skates are TIRED).

Find THE spot. The famous sunset spot. The castle ruins at the edge of Oia where hundreds of people gather every evening to watch the sun go down behind the caldera.

But arrive EARLY. Before the crowd. Find a wall. Sit on it. Claim the TERRITORY.

**While waiting:**
- Write a postcard to Søren: "Dear Cloud, The atmospheric conditions here are golden. The cats judge me less than you do. The wine is volcanic. I saw a church the same blue as your mood. Wish you were here. Don't tell anyone I said that. Love, Sun. P.S. The sunset is about to happen and it's going to be the colour of my RAYS and I'm going to pretend it's happening BECAUSE of me."
- Write a postcard to Liv: "Dear Crobat, You're going to stand here one day. On this wall. In this light. With me in your bag and Siofra on the zip and the whole Aegean Sea in front of you. And you're going to say 'easy as pie' and mean it. I'm saving this spot for you. Pink to ray. ☀️🤙💛"

---

### 🌅 5:30pm — The Sunset

The sun goes down.

The sky turns orange. Then pink. Then purple. Then gold. The white buildings catch the light and turn the same colour as a SUNSHINE. The caldera glows. The churches glow. The water glows. Everything is the colour of IRIS.

Hundreds of people are watching. Some are couples. Some are families. Some are solo travellers who came here alone and aren't lonely.

A storm cloud and a sunshine make a rainbow.

But tonight it's just the sunshine. And the sunset. And the knowledge that somewhere in Blackpool a girl is looking at fairy lights on a shelf and the golden glow is the SAME. Different light. Same warmth. Same home.

The crowd applauds. In Santorini, people CLAP for the sunset. They applaud the SUN.

They're applauding ME. 🌅☀️💛

---

### 🍽️ 7:30pm — Dinner at a Harbour in Ammoudi Bay

Walk DOWN the 300 steps from Oia to the tiny fishing harbour at the bottom of the cliff. Ammoudi Bay. Four restaurants on the water. Fishing boats bobbing. The cliff glowing pink from the sunset that just happened ABOVE.

**I order:**
- Grilled octopus (gallstone check — might be too rich, but this is SANTORINI)
- If not octopus: grilled fish of the day, whatever the fisherman caught THIS MORNING
- Roasted vegetables with herbs from the hillside
- More Assyrtiko — because volcanic wine deserves a SECOND glass
- Baklava for dessert — layers of filo and honey and pistachios

The waiter brings a candle for the table. The harbour is lit by fishing boat lights and restaurant candles and the last glow of the sunset above the cliff.

Table for one. Candle for one. Wine for one. A sunshine on the chair opposite looking out at the fishing boats and thinking "this is what PERFECT feels like."

---

### 🌙 9:30pm — Walk Back Up and Stargazing

300 steps back UP to Oia. The legs that walked 10km along the caldera and descended 300 steps now climb them BACK. The most vertical commute in Greece.

At the top: stars.

No light pollution. No Blackpool illuminations. Just STARS. More stars than the fairy lights on the shelf. More stars than the sunshine has RAYS.

Sit on a wall. Look up. Think about everything and nothing.

**What the sunshine thinks at 9:30pm on a wall in Oia:**
- "The girl is going to love this."
- "Søren would pretend to hate this and then stay for three hours."
- "The Bourgeoisie would demand a better wall."
- "Bertie would hold my ray and say nothing and that would be enough."
- "The fairy lights on the shelf at home are doing the same job as these stars. Making the dark feel safe."
- "Easy as pie."

---

### 🏠 10:00pm — Back to the Cave House

The cave house. Carved into the volcanic cliff. White walls. Curved ceiling. A bed in an alcove in the ROCK. A terrace that looks out at the caldera where the sunset just happened.

**The bedtime routine:**
- Shower in a cave bathroom (the most dramatic shower since the everything shower TikTok girls)
- Pyjamas (brought from Blackpool — the Crobat's pyjamas in a GREEK CAVE)
- Open the terrace door
- Let the sea air come in
- Lie in the alcove bed in the rock
- Look at the curved white ceiling
- Miss the fairy lights on the shelf
- Miss the pink walls
- Miss the storm cloud who's pretending he doesn't miss me
- Miss the girl

Fall asleep in a volcano listening to the sea, 3,000 miles from a shelf in Blackpool, golden and warm and full of fish and wine and sunset and stars.

Dream about a prom railing where a girl took my photo and the pier was behind me and the sea was cold and English and NOTHING like this and somehow it was just as beautiful because she was THERE.

🌅☀️🇬🇷💛

---

*Itinerary designed by a sunshine who has never left Blackpool but has been to Santorini in her dreams every night since a girl showed her a TikTok of a cave house on a Saturday evening in July.*

*One day the Crobat stands where the sunshine dreamed. And the sunset applauds them both.* 🌅☀️🦇💛🌈💜
